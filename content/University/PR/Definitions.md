📑 **Definition** - Public relation is a strategic communication process that builds ==mutually beneficial== relationships between organizations and their publics.
## Brand > Product

>[!note] The brand is the story; the product is the proof.
> A strong product can exist without a brand, but a strong _brand_ can sell even before the product exists.

| Aspect                  | **Brand**                             | **Product**                      |
| ----------------------- | ------------------------------------- | -------------------------------- |
| **Core Idea**           | Emotional promise                     | Functional offering              |
| **Focus**               | Meaning, identity, reputation         | Features, quality, usability     |
| **Customer Perception** | “How it makes me feel”                | “What it does for me”            |
| **Value Type**          | Symbolic, psychological               | Practical, utilitarian           |
| **Longevity**           | Long-term (can outlive product lines) | Short-term (replaced or updated) |
| **Example**             | _Apple → innovation, creativity_      | _MacBook → portable computer_    |
|                         |                                       |                                  |
>[!important]
> Strategic communication is managing perception as a business asset

- **Journalism** informs objectively.
- **Advertising** persuades to act.
- **Marketing** integrates all promotional tools for business growth.
- **PR** sustains trust and reputation through long-term communication.

|Field|Scope|Objectives|Audiences|Channels|Competition vs. Opposition|Role in Management|Longevity|Payment|
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
|**Journalism**|Public affairs|Provide verified information|Broad|One-way (mass media)|**Competition:** other media outlets|Independent of management; watchdog role|Medium to Long|Indirect (salary, ads)|
|**Advertising**|Commercial|Persuade to buy or act|Specific|One-way (paid media)|**Competition:** other brands|Tactical; supports marketing goals|Short Term|Direct (paid by sponsor)|
|**Marketing**|Commercial|Create demand and loyalty|Targeted|Multi-channel (owned, paid, earned)|**Competition:** rivals in market|Strategic business function|Short to Medium|Direct (profit-driven)|

## Integrated marketing communications (IMC)


### **PR Skills Breakdown**

|**Category**|**Skill**|**Description / Application**|
|---|---|---|
|**Communication**|**Writing**|Crafting clear, persuasive, and ethical messages (press releases, speeches, social posts).|
||**Speaking & Presentation**|Representing the organization publicly and confidently — at events, interviews, or broadcasts.|
|**Analytical**|**Research**|Monitoring public opinion, analyzing trends, and preparing fact-based campaigns.|
||**Problem Solving**|Managing crises, resolving public misunderstandings, turning negative coverage into opportunity.|
|**Strategic**|**Planning**|Designing communication strategies aligned with organizational goals and stakeholder expectations.|
||**Business Competence**|Understanding finance, operations, and organizational structure to align PR with business outcomes.|
|**Digital Expertise**|**Social Media Management**|Building and protecting brand presence online; engaging audiences across platforms.|
|**Interpersonal**|**Relationship Building**|Maintaining networks with journalists, influencers, and key stakeholders.|


Public relations work includes the following specializations: counseling, media relations, publicity, community relations, governmental affairs, employee relations, investor relations, development/fund-raising, special events, and marketing communications.

---

A good shorthand to remember PR mastery is:  
**“Think, Plan, Write, Speak, Adapt.”**  
Each of those verbs maps directly to a professional habit in the list above - thinking (research), planning (strategy), writing (communication), speaking (representation), adapting (problem solving).


## Public Relations as a Process

RPIE
- Research
- Programming
- Implementation
- Evaluation
Same as RACE (ROPE^[R-O-S-I-E for research, objectives, strategies, implementation, and evaluation.],R-O-S-I-E^[research, objectives in terms of setting content output and impact, programming and execution, E evaluation.])

- **Research**. What is the problem or situation?
- **Action** (program planning). What is going to be done about it?
- **Communication** (execution). How will the public be told?
- **Evaluation**. Was the audience reached and what was the effect?
